Kerala’s iconic backwaters, especially in Alleppey and Kumarakom, offer mesmerizing houseboat cruises through serene lagoons, coconut groves, and lush landscapes.

  • Varkala Beach – Famous for its stunning cliffs and spiritual vibe.
  • Kovalam Beach – A paradise for beach lovers and surfers.
  • Marari Beach – A tranquil beach known for its golden sands.

Kerala’s tea plantations, especially in Munnar, offer breathtaking views of rolling hills covered in lush green tea bushes. Visitors can explore the plantations and learn about tea production.

  • Appam and Stew – Soft rice pancakes served with a flavorful stew.
  • Fish Curry – Kerala’s signature dish, often made with coconut milk.
  • Sadya – A traditional feast served on a banana leaf, featuring a variety of dishes.
  • Kathakali – A classical dance form known for dramatic expressions.
  • Mohiniyattam – Graceful dance rooted in Kerala’s heritage.
  • Theyyam – A unique ritualistic performance with vibrant costumes.
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary – Home to elephants, tigers, and diverse flora.
  • Silent Valley National Park – A UNESCO World Heritage Site with rich biodiversity.
  • Parambikulam Tiger Reserve – A haven for wildlife enthusiasts.
